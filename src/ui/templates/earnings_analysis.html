How to Read This Chart

  • Yellow Line: Average price movement across ALL earnings days (no filter)
  • Blue Line: Average price movement for earnings days that passed the momentum filter
  • Green Line: Average price movement for earnings days that actually created entry signals (strictest criteria)
  • Shaded Area (±1 Std Dev): Typical range of price movement for filter-passed days (68% of cases fall within this range)
  • 100% = 9:00 Price: All prices normalized to 9:00 opening price as baseline
  • Above 100%: Stock gained value since 9:00
  • Below 100%: Stock lost value since 9:00

What's the Difference?

  • All Earnings (Yellow): Every single earnings day, regardless of quality - the baseline average
  • Passed Filter (Blue): Earnings days where the stock met momentum screening criteria (volume, price movement, etc.)
  • Created Signal (Green): Subset of filter-passed days where an actual entry signal was triggered (watchlist inclusion, timing, etc.)
  • Why compare all three?
    • If blue > yellow: Your momentum filter selects better-performing earnings days
    • If green > blue: Your signal creation logic adds value beyond just the filter
    • If green ≤ yellow: Your filtering/signaling may need improvement
